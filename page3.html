Key Observations

  • GapAlberta consistently shows the largest wage gap, driven by its resource-sector workforce where male-dominated roles command premium wages.
  • GapPrince Edward Island and New Brunswick have the smallest gaps - in some periods women actually out-earn men in these provinces.
  • TrendThe national gender wage gap has gradually narrowed since 2006, with more visible convergence after 2015 as female participation in higher-wage industries increased.
  • TrendDuring COVID-19 (2020), the gap temporarily shrank as male-dominated sectors like construction and resources absorbed larger employment shocks than female-dominated services.
