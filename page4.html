Key Observations

  • RegionalPrairie provinces (AB, SK, MB) consistently log the longest workweeks, reflecting their resource and agricultural industry mix where full-time hours skew higher.
  • RegionalQuebec workers tend to put in fewer hours than the national average, a pattern tied to its stronger unionization rates and distinct labour market culture.
  • GenderMen consistently work more hours per week than women across all provinces and periods, with the gap most pronounced in resource-heavy provinces like Alberta and Saskatchewan.
  • ShockCOVID-19 (2020-H1) is visible as a darker band across nearly all provinces, as reduced economic activity temporarily compressed average hours for those who remained employed.
